Finding Your Ski Level

Skiing is so much more fun when you’re on the slopes with other skiers of a similar ability – not feeling under pressure from the faster or more adventurous, nor feeling as if you have to hold back for the slower and more cautious. In order to help us put together groups that enable everyone to enjoy their skiing, we ask you to read the following carefully and decide which level corresponds to your ability.

1. Beginner
You have never skied before.

2. Novice
You have been skiing a few times and feel comfortable skiing all green and most blue runs. However, you don’t like to ski fast, especially when the visibility is poor, when there is ice or bumps on the pistes, or when they get crowded. Note that if you started skiing some years ago, went once or twice and then had a break, it may be best to join the Beginners' group.

3. Careful Intermediate
You have been skiing a number of times and are comfortable on all blue and easy red runs, provided that the runs have been groomed and the visibility is good. However, when conditions become more difficult you tend to struggle: heavier snow, ice, bumps, narrow runs, crowds can undermine your confidence.

4. Steady Intermediate
You ski parallel turns at a reasonable speed on all red runs, provided that the conditions are good. You find it harder to maintain your rhythm or pace if the runs have not been groomed or become icy and bumpy.

5. Adventurous Intermediate
You are comfortable skiing at speed and happy on moderate black runs with moderate bumps. You like to venture off the piste, as long as the snow conditions and visibility are good. You are not yet comfortable skiing in deep powder on steeper slopes. You would like to improve your technique in all snow conditions on and off piste.

6. Advanced
You have many years’ experience and you are keen to push the boundaries – steeper, narrower, more bumps. You would like to perfect your technique on all terrains. If the conditions permit, you would be keen to venture off into the powder on steeper slopes with the security of being accompanied by an instructor.
